# Taxed HQ Personas and Pain Points

## Audience Slice
Primary segment: W-2 young professionals (ages ~22-35) in their first 1-8 years of career growth.

## Persona 1: First-Year Full-Time Employee
- **Profile:** Recently transitioned from student/part-time work to first full-time W-2 job.
- **Context:** Filing alone for the first time, limited financial literacy, little trust in tax software explanations.
- **Top pain points:**
  - Does not understand why paycheck net is much lower than gross.
  - Unsure how to choose withholding settings on W-4.
  - Confuses refund size with tax efficiency.
  - Fears making mistakes and getting IRS penalties.
- **What they need from Taxed HQ:**
  - Plain-language paycheck decomposition.
  - Guided onboarding and confidence-building simulations.
  - Quick \"what to change now\" action list.

## Persona 2: Promotion and Bonus Professional
- **Profile:** Early-mid career worker receiving salary raises, annual bonuses, and possible equity vesting.
- **Context:** Notices diminishing marginal net income after raises and thinks payroll is \"wrong.\"
- **Top pain points:**
  - Believes a raise can push all income into a higher bracket.
  - Does not understand supplemental wage withholding on bonuses.
  - Cannot estimate monthly cash-flow impact of compensation changes.
  - Lacks a tool to compare raise vs bonus vs RSU vest outcomes.
- **What they need from Taxed HQ:**
  - Side-by-side scenario comparison.
  - \"Why changed\" explanations tied to bracket mechanics.
  - Quick strategies for withholding calibration and quarterly planning.

## Persona 3: W-2 + Side Income Builder
- **Profile:** W-2 employee with growing side income (freelance/creator/consulting) but not full-time self-employed.
- **Context:** Mixed income introduces estimated tax complexity and deductible expense decisions.
- **Top pain points:**
  - Unsure when side income triggers estimated tax obligations.
  - Unsure what is deductible vs risky.
  - Does not know how W-2 withholding can offset side-income tax burden.
  - Feels overwhelmed by combining multiple income streams.
- **What they need from Taxed HQ:**
  - Mixed-income project templates.
  - Guardrails for estimated tax planning.
  - Tax-risk flags with confidence scores and checklist-based remediation.

## Ranked Pain-Point Themes (Across All Personas)
1. **Translation gap:** Numbers exist, but users cannot interpret them.
2. **Scenario blindness:** Users cannot compare choices before acting.
3. **Withholding uncertainty:** W-4 and paycheck settings feel opaque.
4. **Confidence deficit:** Users fear being wrong and avoid action.
5. **Speed problem:** Existing learning paths are too slow and non-interactive.

## Product Implications
- Make scenario comparison the core interaction, not a secondary feature.
- Keep outputs educational and action-oriented (no individualized tax advice).
- Prioritize time-to-insight under 10 minutes for first meaningful outcome.
- Build progress indicators around confidence gain, not just content completion.
